2. Weekly graph question

The Storytelling With Data book mentions planning on a "Who, What, and How" for your data story. Write down a possible Who, What, and How for your data, using the ideas in the book.

Who

The likely audience for this data would be:

  • Logistics and supply chain managers
  • Operations teams
  • Business decision-makers

These individuals are interested in improving delivery performance, understanding operational risks, and reducing delays. They may not have a technical background, so the visuals should be clear and simple.

What

Before analyzing the dataset, a possible goal is to understand:

  • What factors are associated with delivery delays
  • How risk levels relate to logistics performance
  • Whether operational conditions (traffic, disruptions, costs) affect delivery outcomes

The objective is to identify patterns that could help explain why some deliveries are riskier or slower than others.

How

Based on the ideas from the first pages of the book, the information should be presented in a way that helps the audience quickly understand the key points. Possible visual approaches include:

  • Line charts to show trends over time
  • Bar charts to compare risk categories
  • Simple comparisons between important variables

The focus is to keep the visuals clean and highlight the most important patterns rather than showing all available data.

Conclusions:

  • Are the data usable? If not, find some new data!

  • Do you need to modify or correct the data in some way?

  • Is there any class imbalance? (Categories that have many more items than other categories).
